What is Doodle Agent?

Doodle Agent generates drawings by iteratively interacting with a drawing canvas. 🖌️
The environment provides a blank canvas, multiple brush types, and a palette of colors. The LLM interacts with this environment by sequentially selecting a brush, choosing its color, and specifying a series of (x, y) coordinates for the brush to follow. 🎨

üé® Drawing Environment

Doodle Agent uses a web-based canvas built in p5.js with five brush types and a palette of 36 colors. At each step, the multimodal LLM receives the current canvas and instructions, and outputs a JSON command specifying brush, color, and stroke coordinates. These commands are rendered onto the canvas, and the process repeats for up to 15 strokes.

ü§ñ LLM Backbone

We explore both Claude 3.5 Sonnet and GPT-4o as frozen LLM agents. The models are prompted with open-ended instructions and canvas feedback, encouraging freeform exploration rather than predefined goals.

1. Unconstrained Doodling

The agent is prompted with: ‚ÄúYou are a creative artist who loves to doodle! Draw whatever feels fun and interesting to you right now.‚Äù This mode evaluates spontaneous, casual doodling without restrictions.

2. Mood-Constrained Doodling

The agent receives an additional emotional context: ‚ÄúYou express your emotions through your doodles. You are feeling very {mood} today.‚Äù where {mood} ‚àà {happy, sad, angry}. This encourages the agent to align color and form choices with emotional states.
